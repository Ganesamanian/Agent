Issue:  
A property has marked a reservation as a no-show, but the guest claims they cancelled the booking. This discrepancy requires verification of the cancellation timeline and payment details to determine the correct status and any applicable refunds.

Internal policy:  
- Support must verify the timeline of the guest’s cancellation against the property’s no-show report and cancellation policy.  
- If the guest cancelled within the free-cancellation window and the booking was not marked no-show, the charge may be refundable. Support should collect the cancellation timestamp, property policy, and payment ownership details.  
- It is essential to determine who collected the payment. If the property charged the guest directly, support must obtain the payment receipt, message trail, and cancellation timeline before proceeding.  
- Any unresolved conflicts after verification should be escalated internally for resolution.

Public policy:  
- Booking.com allows properties to report guest no-shows through their partner account to manage overbookings and avoid penalties.  
- Hosts can dispute invoices and commissions if they identify errors or discrepancies in charges from Booking.com.  
- These procedures help ensure transparent handling of no-shows and payment disputes.

Recommended action:  
- Collect and review the guest’s cancellation timestamp, the property’s no-show report, and the applicable cancellation policy to verify the timeline.  
- Confirm who processed the payment; if the property charged directly, request the payment receipt and communication records between guest and property.  
- Compare all information to identify discrepancies regarding cancellation and no-show status.  
- If conflicts persist, escalate the case internally and advise the property to use the Booking.com partner portal to report no-shows or dispute invoices as needed.

Next steps:  
1. Request the guest’s cancellation timestamp and the property’s no-show report along with the cancellation policy.  
2. Verify payment ownership and, if applicable, obtain the payment receipt and message trail from the property.  
3. Analyze all collected data to resolve the timeline and payment status.  
4. If the issue remains unresolved, escalate internally and guide the property to report no-shows or dispute invoices via the Booking.com partner portal.
