Issue:  
A property has marked a booking as a no-show after the guest claims they cancelled the reservation. There is a need to verify the accuracy of these claims and determine the appropriate refund or dispute resolution steps.

Internal policy:  
- Support must verify the timeline of the guest’s cancellation versus the property’s no-show marking; any conflicts should be escalated for further review.  
- If the guest cancelled within the free-cancellation window and the booking was not marked no-show at that time, the charge may be refundable. Support should collect the cancellation timestamp, property policy, and payment details before responding.  
- It is essential to determine who collected payment (Booking.com or the property). If the property charged directly, support must obtain the payment receipt, message trail, and cancellation timeline to assess refund eligibility.

Public policy:  
- Booking.com allows properties to report guests as no-shows through their partner platform to manage attendance issues.  
- Properties can request removal of guest reviews that violate content guidelines via the Booking.com partner help center.  
- Clear procedures and step-by-step instructions are available to partners for reporting no-shows and requesting review removals.

Recommended action:  
- Collect and verify the guest’s cancellation timestamp, the property’s no-show marking time, and all relevant communications to identify any timeline discrepancies.  
- Confirm who processed the payment and, if the property charged directly, request payment receipts and message trails to evaluate refund eligibility.  
- If discrepancies or policy violations are found, escalate the case to the legal or refunds team.  
- Advise the property to use the Booking.com partner platform for reporting no-shows and guide them on requesting guest review removals per official guidelines.
