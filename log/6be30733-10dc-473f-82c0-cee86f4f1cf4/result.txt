Issue:
A guest claims to have cancelled their booking, but the property has marked the reservation as a no-show.

Internal policy:
No specific internal policy was provided regarding handling no-shows or cancellations.

Public policy:
Booking.com allows property partners to report guests as no-shows only if the guest fails to arrive without a prior valid cancellation. Reporting a no-show is done through the partner extranet by verifying reservation details and submitting a no-show report. Accurate reporting maintains reservation integrity and impacts guest reviews and future bookings.

Recommended action:
1. Verify the guest’s cancellation status by thoroughly checking the reservation details and any cancellation confirmation in the Booking.com partner extranet.
2. If no valid cancellation is recorded and the guest did not arrive, proceed to report the guest as a no-show through the official Booking.com partner extranet process.
3. Document all communications with the guest and evidence related to their cancellation claim and your verification steps to support your no-show report.
4. If there is any dispute or conflicting information, escalate the matter to Booking.com support or the legal team for further review.

Next steps:
- Review the booking record in the partner extranet to confirm whether the guest’s cancellation was properly registered.
- If no cancellation is found and the guest did not check in, submit the no-show report via the partner extranet.
- Keep detailed records of all interactions and evidence concerning the guest’s claim and your verification.
- Contact Booking.com support if discrepancies remain unresolved or if you need assistance with dispute resolution.
